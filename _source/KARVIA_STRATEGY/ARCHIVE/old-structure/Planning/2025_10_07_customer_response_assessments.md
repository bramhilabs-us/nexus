## 3. What is "SSI Lite"?

**Your Question:** "What is SSI lite?"

**Answer:** Great catch - this wasn't explained clearly in the mockup!

**SSI Lite** is a shorter, faster version of the full assessment:
- **Full SSI**: 47 questions, balanced across all three areas (Speed/Strength/Intelligence)
- **SSI Lite**: 20-30 questions, focused on what matters most for specific roles or situations

**Example Use Cases:**
- **Frontline workers** (Drivers, Technicians) who need quick check-ins, not long surveys
- **Busy executives** who want quarterly pulses without spending 30 minutes
- **Specific departments** where you care more about one dimension (e.g., IT teams might focus more on Strength = system stability)

**Fully Customizable:** You decide:
- How many questions (anywhere from 10 to 100+)
- Which dimensions to emphasize (maybe 50% Strength, 30% Speed, 20% Intelligence)
- What to call it ("Quick Check," "Pulse Survey," "SSI Lite," or your own name)

## 5. Adding Text/SMS as an Option

**Your Question:** "Can we add text as an option to send out surveys?"

**Answer:** Absolutely - and this is becoming essential!

**What We'll Add:**
When you create an assessment invitation, you'll see three delivery options:

📧 **Email** (what's in the mockup now)
- Full message with context
- Best for desk workers

💬 **Text/SMS**
- Short message with survey link
- Perfect for drivers, field techs, warehouse staff without regular email access
- Can include: "Hi [Name], please complete your quarterly check-in: [link] - Takes 10 mins"

🔗 **Shareable Link**
- Generate a link you can post anywhere (company chat, bulletin board, etc.)

**How It Works:**
- System uses phone numbers from your employee database
- You can customize the SMS message
- Respects opt-out preferences and communication regulations
- Tracks who received via SMS vs email

**Why This Matters:** Many of your employees (drivers, technicians, foremen in the field) might rarely check email but always have their phones. SMS gets better response rates for these roles.

## 6. One-Time vs. Regularly Scheduled Surveys

**Your Question:** "A lot of these surveys look to be regularly scheduled - is it clear how to plan one-time surveys?"

**Answer:** You're right - this needs to be clearer! Here's what we'll add:

**When Creating an Assessment, You'll Choose:**

**📅 Survey Schedule:**

**○ One-Time Survey**
- Pick a specific due date
- Sends once, closes when everyone completes (or due date passes)
- Example: "New Manager Onboarding Assessment" or "Post-Project Review"

**○ Recurring Survey**
- Weekly (e.g., "Monday Team Pulse")
- Bi-Weekly
- Monthly (e.g., "First Monday of Each Month")
- Quarterly (e.g., "Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4 Check-ins")
- Annually (e.g., "Year-End Review")

**○ Event-Triggered**
- Automatically send 30 days after hire date
- Send after project completion
- Send when someone moves to a new role

**Visual Clarity:**
- Big toggle at the top: "One-Time" vs "Recurring"
- Calendar picker shows clearly when surveys go out
- Dashboard shows: "Active: 2 one-time, 3 recurring"

**Example Scenarios:**

| Scenario | Type | Frequency |
|----------|------|-----------|
| Annual performance review | One-Time | Set specific date |
| Weekly driver safety check | Recurring | Every Monday |
| New hire 30-day feedback | Event-Triggered | 30 days after hire |
| Quarterly leadership assessment | Recurring | Jan 1, Apr 1, Jul 1, Oct 1 |
| Post-incident review | One-Time | Launch manually after event |
