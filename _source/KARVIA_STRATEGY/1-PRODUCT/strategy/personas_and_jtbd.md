## 🎯 Persona Philosophy

**KARVIA Pro serves SMB teams exclusively (20-200 employees)**. We do NOT serve:
- Solo freelancers (<5 people) — Too small, different needs
- Fortune 500 enterprises (1,000+ employees) — Different tooling requirements
- PM Career Accelerator personas — Different product entirely

**Why SMB Focus?**
- SMBs have **enterprise ambitions** but **startup constraints**
- Need structure without complexity
- Budget-conscious but willing to pay for value
- Want tools that "just work" from day one

## 👥 5 Canonical SMB Personas

### **1. Business Owner / CEO**

#### **Archetype**: Sarah Chen
- **Role**: Founder & CEO
- **Company**: TechFlow Solutions (SaaS startup, 50 employees)
- **Age**: 42
- **Location**: Austin, TX
- **Reports To**: Board of Directors
- **Team Size**: 50 employees (5 departments)

#### **Demographics**
- **Education**: MBA or equivalent experience
- **Experience**: 15+ years in industry, 5+ years as CEO/Founder
- **Tech Comfort**: High (uses Slack, Notion, QuickBooks, HubSpot)
- **Budget Authority**: Final decision maker
- **Salary Range**: $120K-$200K (equity-heavy compensation)

#### **Quote**
> "I need to know if we're on track without spending half my week in status meetings. Enterprise tools are overkill; spreadsheets break when we grow."

#### **Goals**
1. Align all 5 departments to annual strategic plan (3-year vision)
2. Reduce time spent in status meetings (currently 15+ hours/week)
3. Visibility into team health without micromanaging
4. Prove ROI of initiatives to investors/board (quarterly board meetings)
5. Scale operational systems before hiring more managers

#### **Frustrations**
1. **Alignment Chaos**: Departments work in silos, duplicate efforts, miss dependencies
2. **No Single Source of Truth**: "What are our top 3 priorities?" changes depending who you ask
3. **Momentum Loss**: Teams lose energy 3 weeks after planning retreats
4. **Reporting Scrambles**: Board deck prep takes 8+ hours of manual data gathering
5. **Tool Complexity**: Tried Lattice ($500/user/year) but team revolted ("too enterprise-y")
6. **Spreadsheet Hell**: Current Google Sheet has 47 tabs, breaks every quarter

#### **Job-to-be-Done**
> When I'm reviewing company progress, help me see alignment and blockers in <5 minutes so I can make informed decisions without micromanaging.

#### **Success Criteria (How KARVIA Pro Wins)**
- ✅ Company dashboard shows top 3 objectives at a glance
- ✅ All department OKRs visible with progress % (real-time)
- ✅ At-risk objectives surfaced automatically (no need to ask)
- ✅ Export to PDF for board meetings (1-click, <30 seconds)
- ✅ Weekly digest email (automated, no manual prep)
- ✅ Setup took <3 days (not 3 months)

#### **Typical Day (Before KARVIA Pro)**
- **8:00 AM**: Check 5 different Slack channels for status updates
- **9:00 AM**: 1-hour leadership team standup (mostly status updates)
- **10:30 AM**: Follow-up 1:1s asking "Are we on track for Q3 goals?"
- **2:00 PM**: Scramble to update investor report (manual data gathering)
- **5:00 PM**: Leave feeling uncertain if company is aligned

#### **Typical Day (After KARVIA Pro)**
- **8:00 AM**: Open KARVIA Pro dashboard (5 min review, full visibility)
- **9:00 AM**: Leadership standup (30 min, focus on blockers not status)
- **10:00 AM**: Strategic work (dashboard already showed progress)
- **2:00 PM**: Export investor report (1-click PDF, 2 min)
- **5:00 PM**: Leave confident team is aligned, blockers are visible

### **2. Department Manager**

#### **Archetype**: Michael Torres
- **Role**: Head of Product
- **Company**: TechFlow Solutions (same company as Sarah)
- **Age**: 38
- **Location**: Remote (Denver, CO)
- **Reports To**: CEO (Sarah)
- **Team Size**: 15 people (3 product managers, 12 engineers)

#### **Demographics**
- **Education**: BS in Computer Science
- **Experience**: 12 years in product management, 3 years as department head
- **Tech Comfort**: Very high (uses Jira, Figma, Linear, Notion)
- **Budget Authority**: Influencer (recommends, CEO approves)
- **Salary Range**: $110K-$150K

#### **Quote**
> "I need my team working on what actually moves the needle—not what's urgent in Slack. Recognition shouldn't be an afterthought I remember during reviews."

#### **Goals**
1. Cascade company OKRs to department-level initiatives (Q3: "Launch new feature X")
2. Keep team aligned without daily standups (team is remote, async-first)
3. Spot blockers before they escalate (dependencies, resource constraints)
4. Recognize team wins publicly (not just in annual reviews)
5. Report upward confidently (CEO asks "Is Product on track?" weekly)

#### **Frustrations**
1. **Priority Confusion**: Team asks "What should I prioritize?" 5x per day
2. **Spreadsheet Decay**: Product roadmap lives in Google Sheet, no one updates it
3. **Ad-Hoc Recognition**: Remembers to thank people during retros, forgets daily wins
4. **Visibility Gaps**: Can't tell who's overloaded vs underutilized without asking
5. **Manual Reporting**: CEO wants weekly updates, Michael copies from Jira manually
6. **Context Switching**: Team doesn't see how sprint tasks tie to company OKRs

#### **Job-to-be-Done**
> When planning my week, show me team priorities, blockers, and wins so I can lead proactively instead of reactively.

#### **Success Criteria (How KARVIA Pro Wins)**
- ✅ Department OKRs auto-linked to company objectives (clear cascade)
- ✅ Real-time progress visible (no need to Slack "Are we on track?")
- ✅ At-risk objectives highlighted (blockers surfaced early)
- ✅ Recognition prompts (celebrate milestones automatically)
- ✅ Manager dashboard (team performance at a glance)
- ✅ Upward reporting (1-click export for CEO)

#### **User Journey (Quarterly Planning)**

**Before KARVIA Pro**:
1. CEO shares company OKRs in Notion doc (10 pages)
2. Michael translates to department goals in separate spreadsheet
3. Team doesn't see connection between their work and company strategy
4. Weekly status meetings to "keep everyone aligned" (everyone hates them)
5. Quarter ends, realize 40% of OKRs were forgotten

**After KARVIA Pro**:
1. CEO creates company OKRs in KARVIA Pro (visible to all)
2. Michael creates department OKRs, system auto-links to parent objectives
3. Team sees their tasks connected to strategic goals (clear hierarchy)
4. No status meetings (dashboard shows real-time progress)
5. Quarter ends, 70%+ completion rate (auto-tracked, celebrated)

### **3. Team Lead**

#### **Archetype**: Jessica Park
- **Role**: Engineering Team Lead (Frontend Squad)
- **Company**: TechFlow Solutions
- **Age**: 32
- **Location**: Remote (Seattle, WA)
- **Reports To**: Head of Product (Michael)
- **Team Size**: 7 engineers

#### **Demographics**
- **Education**: BS in Computer Science
- **Experience**: 8 years as engineer, 2 years as team lead (first management role)
- **Tech Comfort**: Very high (uses GitHub, Linear, Figma, Vercel)
- **Budget Authority**: None (makes recommendations to manager)
- **Salary Range**: $130K-$160K

#### **Quote**
> "My team ships fast but I'm constantly asked 'How does this tie to our Q3 goals?' I need that link to be obvious, not buried 3 clicks deep."

#### **Goals**
1. Clear sprint-to-OKR alignment (team understands "why" behind tasks)
2. Team knows exactly what "done" looks like (avoid scope creep)
3. Catch burnout signals early (track workload, velocity trends)
4. Celebrate small wins, not just big launches (weekly milestones)
5. Transparent progress for async work (team is distributed, 3 time zones)

#### **Frustrations**
1. **Disconnected Tools**: Jira tasks don't tie to strategic objectives
2. **Context Loss**: Team doesn't see the "why" behind their work
3. **Recognition Fatigue**: Hard to celebrate progress when goals are unclear
4. **Status Update Busywork**: Daily standups feel like reporting theater
5. **Velocity Mystery**: Can't tell if team is ahead, behind, or on track
6. **Async Challenges**: Team in 3 time zones, hard to stay aligned

#### **Job-to-be-Done**
> When my team asks "What should I focus on today?", point them to priorities that clearly ladder up to company OKRs.

#### **Success Criteria (How KARVIA Pro Wins)**
- ✅ Sprint tasks linked to objectives (Jira integration or manual tagging)
- ✅ Progress auto-calculated (team updates KRs, % updates instantly)
- ✅ Team dashboard (everyone sees same priorities)
- ✅ Recognition feed (milestone celebrations visible to all)
- ✅ Async-friendly (no daily standups, dashboard is source of truth)
- ✅ Mobile responsive (team checks progress on phone)

#### **Archetype**: Alex Rivera
- **Role**: Frontend Engineer
- **Company**: TechFlow Solutions
- **Age**: 28
- **Location**: Remote (Miami, FL)
- **Reports To**: Team Lead (Jessica)
- **Team**: Frontend Squad (7 people)

#### **Demographics**
- **Education**: BS in Computer Science
- **Experience**: 5 years as engineer, 2 years at TechFlow
- **Tech Comfort**: Very high (GitHub power user, loves automation)
- **Budget Authority**: None
- **Salary Range**: $95K-$120K

#### **Quote**
> "I work hard, but no one notices. I'm not even sure if my work aligns with the company's strategy. I want to know my impact."

#### **Goals**
1. Understand how my work matters (contribution to company goals)
2. Get recognized when I deliver (not just during annual reviews)
3. See clear path to promotion (OKR completion tied to advancement)
4. Know what "good" looks like (clear success criteria)

#### **Frustrations**

2. **Strategy Mystery**: Doesn't know if daily work ties to company vision

> When I complete a task, show me how it contributed to team/company goals so I feel valued and know my work matters.

- ✅ Task completion updates OKR progress (visible impact)
- ✅ Achievement feed (public recognition when milestones hit)
- ✅ Clear goal hierarchy (see how my work → team OKR → company objective)
- ✅ Performance data (completion rate, velocity trends)
- ✅ Promotion readiness (OKR completion tied to career leveling)
- ✅ Async-first (update KRs on my schedule, not in meetings)

- **Age**: 47
- **Location**: San Francisco, CA

#### **Demographics**
- **Education**: PhD in Organizational Psychology
- **Experience**: 20 years in HR/OD, 8 years as independent consultant
- **Tech Comfort**: Medium-high (uses Notion, Airtable, Calendly)
- **Budget Authority**: Recommends tools to clients (influencer, not buyer)
- **Revenue**: $180K/year (12 retainer clients × $15K/year)

#### **Quote**
> "I help SMBs implement performance systems; I need a tool I can deploy in days, not months. Enterprise tools require certifications; spreadsheets don't scale."

#### **Goals**
1. Deploy OKR platform for clients in <1 week (no 3-month implementations)
2. Template library (don't reinvent the wheel for each client)
3. White-label capability (clients see "Sharma Consulting" branding)
4. Training materials (onboard client teams without hand-holding)
5. Referral revenue (earn commission for tool recommendations)

#### **Frustrations**
1. **Enterprise Tool Complexity**: Lattice requires 40-hour certification to deploy
2. **Spreadsheet Fragility**: Clients outgrow sheets within 6 months, call Priya to rescue
3. **No Templates**: Each client engagement starts from scratch (duplicate work)
4. **Training Overhead**: Spends 20+ hours per client teaching tool (not strategy)
5. **No Revenue Share**: Recommends tools, gets no affiliate commission
6. **Client Churn**: Clients abandon tools after Priya's engagement ends (no stickiness)

#### **Job-to-be-Done**
> When onboarding a new client, give me a turnkey OKR platform I can deploy in <1 week with templates, training, and white-label options.

#### **Success Criteria (How KARVIA Pro Wins)**
- ✅ Turnkey deployment (<5 hours setup, not 40+)
- ✅ Template library (industry-specific OKR templates pre-loaded)
- ✅ White-label capability (Sharma Consulting branding)
- ✅ Training resources (video tutorials, help docs, onboarding wizard)
- ✅ Affiliate program (earn 20% commission on referrals)
- ✅ Multi-tenant support (manage 12 client accounts from one login)

## 📊 User Journey Maps

### **Department Manager Journey: Quarterly Planning**

**Phase 1: Strategic Alignment** (Week 1 of quarter)
- **Before KARVIA Pro**: Read 10-page Notion doc from CEO, translate to department goals in spreadsheet
- **After KARVIA Pro**: CEO creates company OKRs in system, Michael cascades department OKRs with 1-click link

**Phase 2: Team Kickoff** (Week 1-2)
- **Before**: 2-hour meeting to explain department goals, team forgets by Week 3
- **After**: 30-min meeting, team dashboard always visible (no forgetting)

**Phase 3: Execution** (Week 3-12)
- **Before**: Weekly status meetings (1 hour), Slack questions ("Are we on track?"), manual reporting to CEO
- **After**: No status meetings, dashboard shows real-time progress, 1-click export for CEO

**Phase 4: Recognition** (Ongoing)
- **Before**: Ad-hoc Slack kudos, annual review mentions
- **After**: Auto-milestone celebrations, public achievement feed

**Phase 5: Quarter Review** (Week 13)
- **Before**: Scramble to gather data, realize 40% of OKRs were forgotten
- **After**: Historical trend chart, export to PDF, 70%+ completion rate
