# Product Roadmap Review

## Positive Signals
- Roadmap themes (Dynamic OKR Engine, Role-Centric UX, AI Planning Upgrade, Wellbeing Layer, Deployment & Trust) map cleanly to the personas and strategy narrative.
- Quarter-based milestones provide a macro cadence that could help with stakeholder communication once execution catches up.

## Misalignments
1. **Temporal confusion.** Roadmap references releases in Sep/Nov 2024 and Feb 2025, yet current status (Jan 2025) shows Week 0 not started. Either the roadmap is outdated or execution has slipped by multiple quarters. This erodes trust in the roadmap as a forecasting tool.
2. **Dependency blind spots.** Critical prerequisites called out in the audit (shared models, feature flags, invitation system) are absent from the upcoming milestone narrative, despite being blockers for nearly every epic.
3. **Resource ambiguity.** Squads (Platform, Experience, Intelligence) are listed without headcount, capacity, or ownership. Without resourcing assumptions, “5d” estimates per backlog item have no grounding.
4. **GTM integration missing.** No checkpoints for pilot onboarding, legal/compliance, or marketing handoff appear in the roadmap, even though releases rely on partner readiness.
5. **Design/UX backlog unresolved.** Roadmap references empathy-driven flows and action stacks, but there is no linkage to mockup completion or UX debt noted in design review docs.

## Risks
- Stakeholders may continue planning around Release 1/2/3 expectations even though foundational debt makes those targets unattainable without rework.
- Backlog prioritisation could skew toward empathy features before core MVP stability is achieved, leading to an unstable launch experience.
- Missing GTM milestones means the organisation might “ship” features without customer validation or adoption support.

## Recommendations
1. Rebaseline the roadmap starting from the actual Week 0 start date. Classify previously missed releases as lessons learned and plot realistic quarterly targets.
2. Insert architectural prerequisites into the roadmap so leadership sees the cost of stability work (e.g., “Q1 2025 – Platform Hardening” before empathy or AI upgrades).
3. Attach explicit owners and squad capacity assumptions to each milestone. Without this, the roadmap cannot inform staffing or hiring decisions.
4. Add GTM and enablement tracks: pilot recruitment, documentation, compliance, support training, pricing experiments.
5. Create a living roadmap doc that references sprint reviews and audit findings, ensuring it remains grounded in execution reality instead of marketing aspirations.
