# The Company Journey — from first question to playing solo

> Written for the client. The constitution (01) defines the ladder; the Game (03)
> stress-tested it; this document *tells* it — the engagement as the company will
> actually live it, including the uncomfortable middle. Collateral derives from here.

## 1 · The shape of the journey

You climb a staircase of four stages. Each stage does exactly three things: it **starts
on what the last stage handed you**, it **raises one score head-on**, and it **hands you
a deliverable that the next stage stands on**:

| Stage | You arrive with | We raise | You leave with |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Measure** | a sponsor and two weeks | **ARS** — your AI-readiness score | **the Roadmap** |
| **Align** | the Roadmap, committed | **BRQ** — your operating rhythm | **the Cadence** — a team moving in rhythm on the roadmap |
| **Transform** | the Cadence holding | the engine four — **BPI · CFS · FLS · CRS** | **the Measured Engine** — live gauges, outcome records |
| **Evolve** | a measured engine, yours | — | **the equality**: your number and your next move, the same fact in two notations |

At the top, something specific happens: **BOQ ⇄ NBM** — your Business Operating
Quotient (a number) and your Next Best Move (a sentence) become the same truth. The
journey ends the day you stop needing anyone to read your own number to you.

Two facts about the climb, stated up front:

- **A stage is not a grade.** Reaching Transform doesn't mean you transform well — it
  means your productivity is now *measured* instead of guessed. The scores say how good
  you are; the stages say how *real* the scores are. Climbing the ladder is upgrading
  the provenance of your number.
- **The work order is yours, not a curriculum.** The assessment finds your weakest
  driver, and that is where your first program aims (Article 7). An aligned-but-slow
  company does different work than a chaotic one — same ladder, different climb.

## 2 · Before day one — the Sponsor bridge

The journey starts with one decision: **you name a Sponsor** — your player one. The
assessment goes to a person, never to "the company." The Sponsor gets a ten-minute
orientation and does three things inside Nexus: loads the interview matrix (a guided
import, not a spreadsheet emailed around), confirms the sprint dates, names the
tool-inventory contact. When those three are done, the sprint is real.

## 3 · Measure — the two-week sprint

Your people meet Nexus before your executives do: a short, respectful assessment deck —
no chrome, the *why* always on screen — asking how decisions actually route, where
knowledge lives when a teammate leaves. Alongside: floor observation and a tool
inventory. On day 10, the scoring workshop — every human adjustment to a score must cite
its evidence (Article 4: calibrate, never invent).

You receive **ARS** — and it comes the only way any number in this system ever comes
(Article 6): *a score, its band, its provenance label, its anchor pack, and a
drill-down to every signal under it.* Right now the label says **Proxy** — built from
questions and observation, honestly marked as such. With it: **the Roadmap** — your
weakest driver named, and the work that follows from it.

## 4 · Align — the commitment

The Catalyst moment: leadership signs, and the roadmap stops being a document — it
becomes live objectives in Nexus, weakest driver first, with named owners and drafted
key results. This stage is won when something subtle happens: milestone reviews start
landing **on time without anyone prompting**. That is the Cadence — a team moving in
rhythm on a roadmap it owns. It must feel like yours, because it is; a vendor's roadmap
dies at the first reorg.

## 5 · Transform — the valley, owned honestly

Now your teams live in Nexus daily, and your telemetry connects step by step: git, CI,
AI-usage billing, the incident tracker. Each connection arms a gauge.

**Here is the uncomfortable truth, stated before you feel it**: this is where fees peak
and measured proof is thinnest. Gauges that need weeks of data honestly read
*"insufficient signal"* rather than display a guess (Article 3). We call it the proxy
valley, and we own it instead of papering over it:

- **The honest progress metric is "gauges lit: n of 9."** Every gauge declares its
  arming checklist — which connections and how much data it needs. Watching the fog
  lift *is* the product working; the number moves visibly every fortnight even when
  scores can't yet.
- **Your first session subtracts before it adds.** Within a week, your team names one
  standing meeting or status artifact the roll-up has made redundant — and kills it,
  visibly. Adoption is purchased with removal, never with features.
- **The first outcome record is the turn.** When a gauge comes alive — *Measured*, not
  estimated — the numbers are real even when they aren't flattering. Real is the point;
  flattering comes later, with work.

You leave Transform with **the Measured Engine**: live gauges, outcome records with
evidence attached.

## 6 · Handover — and Evolve

**The Guide retires.** Consultant access ends; you keep everything. Not a PDF — the
operating system your company now runs on, gauges live.

What Evolve looks like, lived:

- **Re-assessment on your calendar** re-benchmarks you against the moving frontier. When
  the annual anchor pack updates, your history is **restated** — recomputed from your
  stored signals under the new ruler, never two rulers compared silently. Same signals,
  new ruler, stated openly.
- **The Steward** replaces the Guide — one-to-many, subscription-side. The product runs
  alone (Article 9: no consultant dependency, ever); the Steward adds one human
  touchpoint per cycle, the **Calibration Review**, where the cycle's hardest truth
  arrives with human craft, never via push notification.
- **BRQ becomes measurable** — cadence holding without nudges. The dance.
- **BOQ enters your board deck**, next to revenue, and under it one generated line with
  a confidence and a why (Article 13: a recommendation, never a fact): your weakest
  driver, the recommended move, the projected gain, evidence attached.

You are playing solo. That was the win condition all along. And it doesn't end —
readiness never saturates; the frontier moves every year, and the subscription is the
practice of keeping your number real against it.

**The journey after the journey**: your objectives themselves now climb the same
staircase in miniature — baseline, align, execute, outcome record (the fractal rule).
The staircase you climbed once, your work climbs forever.

## 7 · The gauges-lighting sequence

| Stage | What lights up | Provenance |
|---|---|---|
| Measure | **ARS**, the Baseline, your Organizational Map position | Proxy — honest and labeled |
| Align | **FLS**, **CFS** begin measuring; the Opportunity Register goes live | Partially Measured |
| Transform | **BPI** comes alive — the Eight Metrics velocity pack; knowledge starts compounding | Measured (velocity); quality pack arming |
| Evolve | **BRQ**, the quality metrics — and **BOQ, fully measured, as your North-Star KPI** | Measured |

The lag is design, not defect: each stage raises a score whose measurement matures one
stage later. The gauges are the receipt for the previous stage's work.

## 9 · What we promise — and what we never claim

The promises are structural, not aspirational — each one is a binding article:

- **Every number walks back to evidence.** Tap any score down to the signals that made
  it (Articles 1, 6). A hard truth here is never an opinion.
- **No silent blending.** Proxy, Partially Measured, Measured — always labeled
  (Article 2). Too little data says *"insufficient signal"*, never a guess (Article 3).
- **No fake leagues.** Until enough companies in your segment are genuinely measured,
  you see research-grade anchor placement and your own trend — never a percentile
  against a cohort that doesn't exist.
- **Your data is yours.** Raw signals, forever. BRAMHI receives anonymized,
  segment-aggregated derivatives by contract; re-assessment cadence follows *your*
  calendar (the data covenant).
- **No gauge feeds colleague-vs-colleague comparison.** The opponent is your company's
  own entropy, never your people (the PvE principle). Findings that implicate a person
  render to that person first.
- **Scores aren't predictions.** Scores are auditable arithmetic; recommendations are
  models, and they always arrive with a confidence and a why (Article 13).

And the honest scope line (constitution §9): **BOQ measures your operating engine, not
your market direction.** A company can run a flawless engine and still build the wrong
product. Our next-best-move claim is scoped to operating moves — anyone selling more
than that is overselling, including us.
