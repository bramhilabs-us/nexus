# AI Consulting Playbook — the AIR Strategic Assessment

## Purpose

This is the business card of the pack: how BRAMHI runs a world-class AI transformation consulting engagement, and therefore what Nexus must encode. The AIR framework, the two-week sprint, the deliverables, the pricing, and the positioning live here. The product card decides where each piece surfaces in the UI; the tech card decides how it's implemented (entirely inside the assessment block — modularity is non-negotiable).

**Boundary note**: everything in this playbook that is AIR-specific — dimensions, instruments, scoring, deliverable templates — is *content of one assessment implementation*, not core product. Tomorrow's assessment replaces this playbook's framework section without touching the rest of Nexus.

## TL;DR

- We sell **certainty, not assessments**: "where AI will create measurable value in your business, where it will fail, and what to do next."
- **AIR (AI Readiness Intelligence Rating)** measures five organizational capabilities — Leadership, Workforce, Process, Data, Execution. AIR is the *output*; **the company's actual operating journey is the input**. Evidence comes from observing how the company works, not from dimension-by-dimension interviews.
- The delivery vehicle is a **Two-Week Discovery Sprint** (~35 interviews, on-site, walk-the-floor) producing seven executive deliverables, all generated and housed in Nexus.
- **The assessment is the acquisition engine, not the business**: ~$25k assessment → quick wins → transformation partnership ≈ $130k client value; then handover converts the client into a Nexus product account.
- Target: **the ICP v1 profile** (C-016.5, [ICP.md](ICP.md)): software *product* companies, ~20–500 people, ≥10 engineers, modern delivery pipeline, measurement-permissive jurisdiction; North America first. The earlier "service-heavy SMB" framing is superseded — services-enabled companies are a future anchor pack, not a v1 customer.

## Where AIR sits in the BRAMHI score universe

AIR is the **entry diagnostic** of a larger framework: [scores/BOQ.md](scores/BOQ.md) — the Business Operating Quotient (0–100), the North Star metric a transformed company ultimately adopts (one document per driver lives in `scores/`). The two-week sprint's instruments are **signal collectors** in BOQ terms; the BRAMHI Baseline is the first signal snapshot; cadenced re-assessments keep the signals flowing until BOQ comes online. The engagement sells AIR; the relationship matures into BOQ.

## Positioning

Most AI consulting firms sell workshops, assessments, reports, and training — and clients buy out of fear of missing out. We do not sell an "AI Readiness Assessment" (that sounds like a survey). We sell the **AIR Strategic Assessment™**:

> A two-week executive engagement that identifies where AI can create measurable value, where it will fail, and what the next 12 months should look like.

One sounds like a report. The other sounds like a business decision.

## The qualification gate — before any pitch

[ICP.md](ICP.md) §2 owns the definition; this playbook owns the script. Five questions
at Prospect, before the Sponsor bridge: software product revenue? 20–500 people? ≥10
engineers? modern pipeline (or installable week one)? measurement-permissive
jurisdiction? **Any no = route per the ICP segment registry, kindly**: a misfit segment
scored against `saas-product` anchors gets a wrong number, and we don't sell wrong
numbers. The "no" script: *"our v1 ruler is calibrated for software product companies;
your segment's ruler is on our roadmap — here's what would have to be true."*

**Segment TLO variants**: the headline dollar claim — TLO of $1.4M–$2.5M/year per ten
engineers — is calibrated for the ICP segment only. Collateral and pitches never quote
it to other segments; each future anchor pack derives its own TLO line when the segment
earns its pack (ICP.md §4). One TLO, one segment, honestly.

## The Sponsor bridge — the engagement's first move

The lobby gets a host (03 §6.1; the delegate path per 04_RUNTIME_MODEL §4). **Add
Client captures a named Sponsor** — the client's player one. The auto-initiated AIR
mail goes to that *person*, who **takes the assessment or delegates it** (delegation is
a designed move, not a failure). Sponsor onboarding is flashcard-grade — they are the
engagement's first conviction. Their first three actions happen inside Nexus: load the
interview matrix on Teams (a guided import, never an emailed spreadsheet), confirm the
sprint dates, name the tool-inventory contact. Prospect's exit is crisp: **sponsor
activated + matrix loaded + sprint scheduled.**

**The engagement nudge chain** (04_RUNTIME_MODEL §4): every transition has a designed
trigger and a fallback — system reminder first, the consultant's human nudge second,
never the reverse. The consultant's nudges are scarce by design: structure motivates,
nudges catch. Every nudge (system or human) is logged — nudge-independence is a BRQ
signal, and the engagement's own send-rate declining is part of the proof the cadence
is forming.

## The AIR framework

AIR is not measuring AI. AIR measures the five hidden organizational capabilities that determine whether AI becomes a toy, a pilot, a department tool, a company capability, or a strategic advantage:

| Dimension | Measures | Evidence source (sprint day) | Backward-mapping questions |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Leadership** | Strategic alignment, governance, sponsorship, investment commitment | Day 1 | Is there an AI strategy? Is budget allocated? Is leadership aligned? |
| **Workforce** | Capability, adoption, confidence, learning culture | Day 7 | Can employees use AI? Are they willing to? |
| **Process** | Maturity, documentation, standardization, automation potential | Days 2–4 | Are workflows documented? Standardized? |
| **Data** | Information quality, accessibility, governance, organizational knowledge | Day 5 | Is data accessible? Is knowledge structured? |
| **Execution** | Ability to turn initiatives into measurable outcomes | Days 6–9 | Can initiatives become outcomes? |

**The design principle**: the sprint is organized around how the company operates (operations, sales, service, technology, finance, people) — *not* around AIR dimensions. The AIR Score is a byproduct of understanding the organization. This kills meeting fatigue and duplicate interviews, and makes the score credible because it rests on observed reality, not self-reported surveys.

## The Two-Week Discovery Sprint

### Week 1 — Discovery

| Day | Activity | Participants | Deliverable artifact |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 AM | Executive Alignment Workshop (3h): business model, revenue drivers, growth strategy, challenges, AI expectations | CEO, COO, CFO, CTO/CIO, VP Ops, VP Sales | **Business Context Canvas** |
| 1 PM | Leadership interviews (60 min each): AI vision, governance, investment appetite, risk tolerance | CEO, COO, CTO | **Leadership Readiness Assessment** |
| 2 | Operations deep dive — *walk the floor, don't stay in meeting rooms*: process steps, bottlenecks, approvals, rework, manual effort, waiting time | Ops director, supervisors, team leads | **Value Stream Map** |
| 3 | Sales & customer journey: lead gen, qualification, proposals, follow-up, CRM usage, lost opportunities | Sales director, SDRs, AMs | **Revenue Flow Map** |
| 4 | Customer service: ticket volumes, resolution time, repeated issues, knowledge gaps, manual tasks | Service manager, agents, escalation | **Customer Experience Map** |
| 5 | Technology & data: systems inventory (CRM, ERP, SharePoint, email, ticketing, DBs). Where is knowledge? Where is truth? Where is duplication? | IT, engineering, data owners | **Enterprise Knowledge Map** |

### Week 2 — Validation & opportunity mapping

| Day | Activity | Participants | Deliverable artifact |
|---|---|---|---|
| 6 | Finance workshop: labor/process/operational costs, revenue leakage, AI opportunity economics | CFO, controllers | **Value Realization Model** |
| 7 | Workforce assessment: AI literacy, adoption, trust, resistance, skills | 20–30 employees across departments | **Workforce Readiness Index** |
| 8 | Department opportunity workshops — the key question: *"If you had a perfect digital employee tomorrow, what work would you delegate first?"* Each use case scored: effort, impact, complexity, ROI | One workshop per department | **AI Opportunity Register** |
| 9 | Validation workshop: present findings, validate assumptions, remove bias (most assessments fail because consultants assume) | Leadership team | **Confirmed Organizational Reality Model** |
| 10 | AIR scoring workshop | Leadership, department heads, assessment team | **AIR Score™** |

### Interview matrix (50–100 employee company)

~35 people gives statistical confidence; interviewing 50 is the classic consultant mistake.

| Role | Count |
|---|---|
| CEO / COO / CFO / CTO-IT | 1 each |
| Department heads | 4–6 |
| Team leads | 5–8 |
| Employees | 20–30 |

## The deliverables

What the client receives at day 10 — every one of these is a Nexus artifact, generated by the AIR assessment block, presented from Nexus (no parallel slide-deck universe):

1. **AIR Score™** + department readiness breakdown
2. **AI Opportunity Register** — use cases ranked by impact, complexity, effort, ROI
3. **AI Risk Register** — barriers and adoption risks
4. **Quick Wins / 90-Day Action Plan** — seeded into Nexus as the first objectives
5. **12-Month Transformation Roadmap** — the program plan for the engagement's next phase
6. **BRAMHI Baseline** — the longitudinal benchmark for re-assessment
7. **Executive report + presentation + leadership workshop**

### The proxy-valley script — owning the uncomfortable middle

Fees peak during Align–Transform exactly when measured proof is thinnest (constitution
§9; 03 F2). The engagement script owns this *before the client feels it*, at the Align
signing:

1. **Name it on day one of Align**: "for the next N weeks your gauges will honestly say
   *insufficient signal* while your telemetry connects — that honesty is why the number
   can be trusted at the end."
2. **Sell instrumentation as the mid-game deliverable**: the client is buying
   visibility, and watching the fog lift *is* the product working. The honest progress
   metric is **"gauges lit: n of 9"** — it moves visibly every fortnight even when
   scores can't.
3. **The turn is the first Measured gauge**: when BPI comes alive — real numbers, not
   flattering ones — the script says *real is the point; flattering comes later, with
   work*.
4. **Never bridge the valley with estimates.** A confident number before its floor is
   met would be a violation of Article 3 and the end of the trust the engagement sells.

### The data covenant — stated before anyone asks

In the engagement contract from day one (03 F12): **the client owns their signals, raw
and forever**; BRAMHI receives anonymized, segment-aggregated derivatives by contract;
the benchmark flywheel never touches raw client data; re-assessment cadence is set by
the client's calendar, never by BRAMHI's data hunger. Enterprise procurement asks about
this on day one — the answer is already in the contract template.

## Marketing collateral

Two artifacts, maintained alongside this playbook (files to be added under `0-BUSINESS/collateral/` when designed):

1. **The journey document** — reads like the first chapter of a transformation, not a proposal. Narrative arc: The Challenge (everyone says AI changes your industry; nobody can say where it creates value *for you*) → Introducing AIR → the questions we answer (strategy, operations, workforce, tech/data, impact) → the five dimensions → the two-week structure → what you receive → who it's for → outcomes. Cover: *"AIR Strategic Assessment™ — From AI Curiosity to Measurable Business Value. A Two-Week Executive Discovery Sprint. Powered by AIR™ and the BRAMHI Organizational Intelligence Framework™."*
2. **The executive one-pager** — sells the meeting; the journey doc explains. Five visual blocks, minimal words: the journey (Discover → Measure → Prioritize → Execute), what we assess (5 dimensions), what we do (Week 1 understand / Week 2 identify), what you get (5 deliverables), the outcome (AI Confusion → AI Confidence). McKinsey-grade visual hierarchy; matches the Nexus minimalistic design language.
3. **The value bridge** (03 §7) — the board-language translation: each driver mapped to a financial line the board already tracks (BPI → R&D ROI and the cost side of Rule of 40; CFS → opex of coordination; BRQ → forecast reliability; …). One page; every claim cites the segment's anchor pack; the TLO line is the ICP segment's only (§ qualification gate). The client-facing journey narrative source is [COMPANY_JOURNEY.md](COMPANY_JOURNEY.md) — collateral derives from it, never invents a parallel story.
