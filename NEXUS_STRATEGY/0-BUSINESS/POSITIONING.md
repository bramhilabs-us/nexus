# Positioning — what Nexus is, against what, for whom

## The category

**Nexus is a Transformation OS** — the operating system an organization runs its transformation on, then keeps as its project-management OS. Not a consulting deliverable, not another PM tool, not an assessment product: the category is deliberately the intersection, because no incumbent sits there (BOQ_FRAMEWORK § the pitch: every existing tool solves one box of the bridge; nothing manages the whole diagram).

**AI transformation is the launch vertical** (C-006). The category claim is bigger than the vertical on purpose — ESG, digital, ops-excellence transformations are new assessment plugs on the same blocks, not new products.

## The one-liner, per audience

| Audience | One line |
|---|---|
| CEO / economic buyer | "We tell you where AI will create measurable value in your business, where it will fail, and what to do next — then we leave you running the system that does it." |
| The team (managers, workers) | "Strategy as a rhythm, not a deck: your objectives, this week's milestone, today's tasks — progress takes care of itself." |
| Org-direct (no consultant) | "Assess your readiness, turn the results into objectives, run the weekly rhythm — one tool, end to end." |
| Investors / partners | "Consulting is the wedge, the handed-over OS is the moat, and the methodology (AIR → BOQ) is the IP." |

## Differentiation — three axes, one sentence each

| Versus | They leave | We leave |
|---|---|---|
| **AI consultancies** | A PDF and an invoice | **The operating system the team keeps running on** — the engagement ends, the subscription begins (the handover moat, NORTH_STAR) |
| **PM tools** (Asana, Monday, Notion-as-PM) | A task tracker the team must motivate itself to use | A system **seeded by an assessment**, driven by NOF's self-rolling objectives, scored by a framework (AIR → BOQ) — the *why* is built in |
| **Assessment vendors** | A score and a workshop | A score **wired to execution**: deliverables become objectives, objectives roll up from real task hours, outcomes get measured at close (progress ≠ outcome, NOF) |

## The message house

Roof: **"We sell certainty."** (Brand: calm and certain — DESIGN_LANGUAGE.)

| Pillar | Claim | Proof |
|---|---|---|
| **Credible diagnosis** | The AIR Score rests on observed reality, not self-reported surveys | Journey-is-input sprint design; every number walks back to L3 signals (BOQ traceability rule) |
| **Execution that compounds** | Strategy becomes a weekly rhythm the org owns | NOF: 6–7 self-rolling objectives, milestone cadence, automatic roll-up, outcome records |
| **You keep everything** | Handover is a feature, not an exit | Builder mode; Sustained KPIs tracked year over year; 90-day retention is our judged metric (4-CUSTOMER/METRICS) |

## Naming discipline

**AIR Strategic Assessment™** (never "AI readiness survey" — one sounds like a report, the other like a business decision, playbook § positioning) · **AIR Score™** · **NOF** (internal framework name; user-facing copy says "objectives" plainly) · **BOQ** (the North-Star metric a transformed company adopts) · **Nexus** (the product) · **srishti** (the document/intelligence add-on, always lowercase).

## Karvia adaptation note (IM-11 light)

Karvia positioned as a consultant-only OKR platform with SSI scoring. Nexus repositions: category up (Transformation OS), motion widened (org-direct first-class), scoring replaced (SSI dead per C-006; AIR→BOQ is the new spine), and the handover — which Karvia never had — becomes the centerpiece of the story.
