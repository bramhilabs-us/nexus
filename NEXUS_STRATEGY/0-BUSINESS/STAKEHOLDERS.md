# Stakeholders — who must say yes, who must love it

## The map

Two buying-side roles, four playing archetypes (PRODUCT_STRATEGY § roles — permissions bind to archetypes only), one BRAMHI-side role. Per C-005 every player operates inside a Program.

| Stakeholder | Stake | Home page | First-20-seconds question | What loses them |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Economic buyer** (CEO/founder) | Pays for the engagement; adopts BOQ as North Star at end-state | (Dashboard, occasional) | "Where did this number come from, and what did I get for the fee?" | A score that can't walk back to evidence; consulting exhaust instead of outcomes |
| **Champion / Business Owner** (COO/ops VP) | Owns the program day to day; owns Company Profile | **Dashboard** | "Is the program on track, what needs me today?" | Vanity tiles; visibility without an action (the Push CTA exists for them) |
| **Manager** | Turns assessment results into objectives; drives KRs | **Objectives** | "Which of my objectives is slipping, and where do I plan next?" | Manual progress bookkeeping (NOF kills it); calendar ritual (quarters) |
| **Worker** (any IC label — architect, designer, analyst…) | Executes tasks; the most users, the least patience | **Planning** | "What do I do today?" | Gantt-chart density; updating status for someone else's report |
| **Assessment taker** (often accountless) | 6 minutes of their attention — the funnel's widest persona | flashcard deck (no chrome) | "Why am I being asked this, and what happens with my answers?" | Mystery-quiz surveys; never seeing results (the completion card promises both) |
| **Consultant** (BRAMHI side, motion 1 only) | Runs engagements; exists to help the other players play better — never owns the company's game | **My Clients** | "Which engagement needs me, in under 20 seconds?" | Anything that makes them re-enter data the engagement already captured |

## Adoption order (who must love it, in sequence)

1. **The taker** — most client employees' first-ever Nexus moment is the Day-7 deck; if it feels like a chore, the funnel leaks before the buyer ever sees a dashboard (completion + sentiment are judged metrics).
2. **The Worker** — Planning is the highest-frequency surface; weekly-rhythm retention IS Builder-mode retention.
3. **The BO/Manager** — they renew; the Dashboard→Objectives drill loop must convert visibility to action.
4. **The buyer** — they sign; the AIR Score's traceability and the outcome records are their evidence.

The consultant is deliberately *not* in this list: in motion 1 they're on our side of the table, and in motion 2 they don't exist — a product only consultants love fails both motions.

## Org-direct variant

No consultant: the champion self-runs the assessment (Discovery-grade), the buyer and champion are often the same founder, and the taker/Worker/Manager personas are unchanged — which is the point (GTM rule: same pages, no consultant hardcoded in the path).

## Karvia adaptation note

Karvia modeled org→manager→employee + consultant as fixed roles. Nexus keeps exactly four archetypes, makes role labels admin-extensible config (zero code), elevates the *taker* to a first-class persona (Karvia's survey-takers were an afterthought — and it showed in SSI completion), and adds the economic buyer as a distinct evidence-demanding audience (BOQ traceability exists for them).
