# The NBM Model — the staircase and the core USP

> **Status: RATIFIED canon (C-015, 2026-06-11).** Captured from the 2026-06-10 founder
> brainstorm, then stress-tested by a devil's-advocate simulation (Works24 end-to-end + 15
> archetypes; the structure held in all 16 runs — every failure was theory-layer) and
> finalized. The Staircase is now the constitution's signature diagram (01_NEXUS_MODEL.md §4);
> the open items in §5 below are resolved, each with its pointer. Bottom-brick naming:
> the brainstorm's "Discover" canonizes as **Measure** (the tagline's verb).

## 1 · The signature result — the Staircase

One diagram that explains the entire thing. Each stage is a brick with **two faces**: a score
to raise (the quantitative face) and a deliverable to hand over (the qualitative face). The
deliverable of each stage is the floor of the next. The staircase terminates where the two
faces become the same thing.

```
                                          ┌─────────────────────────────────┐
                                 EVOLVE   │   BOQ      ⇄      NBM           │
                                          │   a number        a sentence    │
                                          │   ────  the same truth  ────    │
                                          │   "your next best move is       │
                                          │    directly correlated to       │
                                          │    your operating quotient"     │
                                          └───────────────▲─────────────────┘
                                                          │ enters when the engine is measured
                            ┌─────────────────────────────┴───┐
                  TRANSFORM │  raise:   BPI · CFS · FLS · CRS │
                            │           (the engine, put      │
                            │            to work)             │
                            │  deliver: a measured engine —   │
                            │           live gauges, outcome  │
                            │           records               │
                            └───────────────▲─────────────────┘
                                            │ enters when rhythm holds
              ┌─────────────────────────────┴───┐
        ALIGN │  raise:   BRQ                   │
              │           (set the rhythm       │
              │            between the team)    │
              │  deliver: a team moving in      │
              │           cadence on the        │
              │           roadmap               │
              └───────────────▲─────────────────┘
                              │ enters when the roadmap is committed
┌─────────────────────────────┴───┐
│  raise:   ARS                   │
│           (assess — discover    │
│            where you are)       │
│  deliver: THE ROADMAP           │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
 DISCOVER
```

**The story, as the founder tells it**: It all starts with ARS — the Discovery stage. Based on
the assessment we discover where the company is and produce the AIR Score; the deliverable of
Discovery is **the Roadmap**. Align works on that roadmap, and its head-on metric is **BRQ** —
before chasing productivity, set the rhythm between the team. A team that holds cadence is what
Transform needs — there **BPI, CFS, FLS, CRS are put to work**. A measured engine is what the
ultimate stage needs — where the claim becomes *"your next best move is directly correlated to
your BOQ."*

**The apex is a convergence, not a summit.** At the bottom, the number and the deliverable are
different things (a score vs a document). Each stage up, they get closer (a rhythm score vs a
team in rhythm; engine scores vs a running engine). At the top they are identical: **BOQ is NBM
counted; NBM is BOQ spoken.** One is text, the other is a number — the same truth in two forms,
equal. This gives "self-sustaining" a precise meaning: *a company has arrived when its
measurement and its next move are the same fact in two notations.*

**Structure vs theory** (the founder's requirement: "the structure has to remain perfect while
the right theory comes out"):

| | What it is | May it change? |
|---|---|---|
| **Structure** (constitutional) | A staircase of stages; each stage = *enter on the previous deliverable · raise one focus score head-on · hand over a deliverable that is the next stage's floor*; terminates in the BOQ ⇄ NBM equality | No — this is the shape |
| **Theory** (v1, calibratable) | *Which* score sits in *which* brick (ARS→Discover, BRQ→Align, engine four→Transform); what each deliverable precisely contains | Yes — real engagements calibrate it; swapping a label inside a brick never moves the staircase |

The founder's own flag, recorded: BRQ-at-Align is *"one of the options I'm thinking of — might
not be the right option."* The structure absorbs that uncertainty by design.

## 2 · NBM and BOQ — the relationship, settled through three corrections

The brainstorm converged through three explicit corrections, kept here so the reasoning isn't lost:

1. *Not* NBM above BOQ as the top of the calculation — **a move cannot be calculated**, only
   the certainty that informs it. NBM is qualitative (advice); BOQ is quantitative (arithmetic).
2. *Not* NBM as the summit of a separate qualitative ladder either — NBM is not a final unlock.
3. **Equal.** One is text, one is a number. NBM is present-tense from day one ("TODAY!" — even
   Discovery's Opportunity Register is company-level NBM, proxy-backed). The relationship is
   cyclical, not hierarchical: **higher the BOQ → better the NBM; better the move → higher the
   BOQ.** It's rhythm, not a linear formula. What matures over the cohort is not *whether*
   Nexus gives NBM but *how good* it gets: resolution (company → team → person), trust
   (proxy → measured), assistance (guided → self-sustaining).

**The commercial logic**: NBM is the product; BOQ is the trust certificate. The pitch:
*"Nexus tells you — the company, the team, the person — your next best move."* / *"Why should I
trust it?"* / *"Because of BOQ."* Today the credible deliverable is the AIR Score and the
Roadmap; the future (after ~1000 measured companies) is that it's **known knowledge that Nexus
predicts the next best move for an AI SaaS company** — not just a project-management tool.
*We sell the qualitative; we defend it with the quantitative.*

**Honesty obligation**: until the cohort exists, the BOQ ↔ NBM correlation is a **hypothesis**,
stated as such (now in the constitution's honest limits, §9).

**The causal-correctness requirement** (added at ratification, C-015.7): at company altitude
the v1 NBM falls out of the BOQ arithmetic itself — the geometric mean's defining property
("fixing your weakest driver always helps more") means **BOQ is the position and NBM is the
gradient at that position**. But the gradient must be read **through the Bridge's causal edges**
(ARS enables BRQ enables the engine): drivers are coupled, so the weakest *measured* driver can
be a symptom whose cause sits upstream. Which-driver is computed arithmetic (Nexus); which
specific move within it, for which team and person, is prediction (iBrain) — Article 13 splits
exactly there.

## 3 · The supporting claims (all ratified, C-015)

### 3.1 The duality design law — "calculable categorization is the USP"

At every level, the model projects something messy onto **two orthogonal, measurable axes** —
because two axes are calculable, explainable, and plottable. Calibration leans on the law of
large numbers across the cohort (the quant-banking principle). The grid so far:

| Thing | Its duality | Where it already exists |
|---|---|---|
| The company | rhythm × efficiency | the Organizational Map |
| The engine | speed × quality | the Eight Metrics' two questions |
| A task | thinking × doing | founder notes p.3 |
| A person | action × reaction (+ slow/fast thinking) | founder notes p.3 → the Profile tags |
| A decision | data × gut | founder notes p.1 — the before/after of Nexus itself |
| **The product** | **measure × move (number × text)** | **the Staircase's two faces** |

Frontier LLMs can reason but cannot *measure you* — they lack the instrumented context, the
cohort calibration, and the closed outcome loop. This grid is the moat, stated precisely.

### 3.2 NBM at three altitudes — one function, three zooms

| Altitude | The NBM instance | Surface |
|---|---|---|
| **Company** | which driver/initiative to attack next | the Opportunity Register (= ranked company-NBMs) |
| **Team** | which objective/milestone deserves focus now | NOF state + capacity + dependencies |
| **Person** | which task, to whom, today | Planning page; the task × person match |

**Altitude limits** (added at ratification, C-016): the selling altitude is the company (the
2028 one-liner); the *training* altitude is the person — daily usage is what feeds the engine.
But person-altitude NBM degrades by **org size** (below ~30 people the founder's own knowledge
of every person beats any matcher) and by **jurisdiction** (works-council/consent regimes
constrain per-person telemetry). And it carries the Goodhart exposure: once gauges are felt as
appraisal, they get gamed — the appraisal posture is deliberately open (C-016.4), watched per
score doc, revisited on the first real-world signal.

### 3.3 The task × person match (founder notes p.3, formalized v1)

- **Task**: decomposes into a *thinking slot* and a *doing slot*; categorizes by domain
  (marketing, design, ML…); carries an uncertainty level. Identifying/classifying the task is
  the easy half.
- **Person**: action-oriented vs reaction-oriented; slow vs fast thinking; personality
  (nature/nurture ~49/51) — **discovered by assessment instruments**, which is why the Profile
  is so personal. The Profile's match-grade tags are the person-vector.
- **Match**: task demands map to person traits with weights ("if the action requires the doer
  to be more rational, weightage is added to people who react rationally"). v1 = weighted
  similarity over the two vectors plus context (load, milestone, streak).

### 3.4 The Nexus/iBrain seam — computes vs predicts

**Scores are auditable arithmetic** (deterministic, signal-traceable — Nexus; the trust layer
is never a black box). **Predictions are learned models** (pattern detection, ranking,
forecasting — iBrain; allowed to be probabilistic). Candidate constitutional article: *a score
is never a prediction; an NBM is always displayed as a recommendation with a confidence and a
why — never as a fact.*

### 3.5 The category claim

Every competitor lives on one face only: BI/analytics = numbers without moves; consultants =
advice without instruments (snapshot); frontier LLMs = fluent advice, zero measurement of you;
PM tools = tracking without either. **Nexus is the only thing that runs both faces and welds
them stage by stage.** Vision sentence kept verbatim-in-spirit: *part management tool, part
sounding board, part strategic tool, part collaboration tool.*
